Study Disclosure & Limitations
This report presents outcomes from an observational cohort study of 503 overweight adults who followed a licensed predecessor formulation to NutraGLP® Sync under real-world conditions over six months. This was not a randomized, placebo-controlled clinical trial. The absence of a control group, randomization, or blinding means that factors other than the formulation may have contributed to observed outcomes. Results should not be interpreted as proof of causation.

Adverse event data was captured via participant self-report without systematic follow-up protocols or independent clinical verification. This method is known to undercount adverse events relative to prospective clinical trial monitoring. Comparisons to adverse event rates for semaglutide and tirzepatide are drawn from published randomized controlled trial meta-analyses and real-world EHR cohort data (Rodriguez et al., JAMA Internal Medicine, 2024), which use substantially more rigorous adverse event collection methodologies. These comparisons are presented for context and are not direct head-to-head comparisons.

Weight loss comparisons to semaglutide and tirzepatide are derived from independent published clinical literature, not from a controlled study conducted alongside this cohort. Differences in study design, participant characteristics, dosing protocols, and outcome measurement methods limit the comparability of these figures. Observed weight loss outcomes in this cohort may not be reproducible in controlled trial settings. Individual results will vary significantly based on adherence, baseline health status, diet, activity, and other factors.

This observational data is hypothesis-generating and intended to support the design of future controlled clinical research. A placebo-controlled, randomized trial is planned as a primary use of proceeds. Nothing in this report constitutes a medical claim, a guarantee of results, or FDA-evaluated evidence of safety or efficacy. NutraGLP® products are dietary supplements and are not intended to diagnose, treat, cure, or prevent any disease. Consult a qualified healthcare provider before beginning any weight management program.
